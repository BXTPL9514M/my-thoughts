A World Without Rain, But Full of Lightning

There is something strangely beautiful about the weather when there is no rain, yet lightning fills the sky. No clouds above us, but still a powerful force moving silently. It makes me think about how the world works beneath the surface—about things we feel but never fully understand.

I believe that all of us are connected in ways we don’t talk about. Not through words, but through thoughts, patterns, and shared fears. Yet we never try to go deeper into this connection. Maybe not because we can’t, but because we are quietly discouraged from doing so.

The world we live in feels guided by people who understand these systems far better than we do. They don’t need violence or fear to control us. They use structure, routine, and dependence. These are the real “dark knights” of the modern world—not villains in shadows, but forces hidden in plain sight.

We are placed inside an invisible box. Not a prison with walls, but one made of expectations. Inside it, we work, compete, and move in circles— like rats chasing bait that always stays just out of reach. We are told this is life.

Most of us never truly see the world for what it is. And sometimes I fear that we never will. Not because escape is impossible, but because it is made to feel unnecessary, unrealistic, or dangerous.

Even our parents are part of this system. Not because they want to harm us, but because their own lives were shaped the same way. When our future is placed in their hands, and they are already trapped in the cycle, the cycle continues. We are sent to schools not to question the system, but to learn how to function inside it.

Think about it.

— Mohammad Laxmidhar
